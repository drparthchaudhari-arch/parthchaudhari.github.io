Canine and Feline Congestive Heart Failure: Clinical Approach

CHF management requires integrating staging, medication strategy, and monitoring priorities. This guide covers species differences, decompensation recognition, and practical treatment sequencing for exam success and clinical application.

Rapid Algorithm

  • Recognize respiratory distress patterns consistent with pulmonary edema or pleural effusion from cardiac origin.
  • Assess oxygen needs and provide supportive care while gathering diagnostic context.
  • Determine ACVIM stage to guide acute and chronic management decisions.
  • Initiate decongestive therapy appropriate to species and presentation severity.
  • Establish monitoring plan with clear escalation and follow-up intervals.

Diagnostic Flow

  • Thoracic imaging confirms congestion pattern and helps differentiate cardiac from non-cardiac causes.
  • Echocardiography characterizes underlying structural disease and guides prognosis.
  • ECG identifies rhythm disturbances that may complicate management.
  • Blood pressure and renal markers inform medication safety and tolerance.
  • NT-proBNP can support diagnostic uncertainty in select cases.

Treatment Flow

  • Oxygen and stress reduction are first priorities in acute decompensation.
  • Diuretic strategy varies by species: cats often need pleural drainage; dogs typically respond to furosemide.
  • Pimobendan benefits select stages and should be considered early in appropriate candidates.
  • Monitor electrolytes, renal values, and blood pressure during active treatment phases.
  • Plan transition to chronic therapy with owner education on monitoring and recheck timing.

Exam Traps

  • Treating cats identically to dogs misses important species-specific physiology.
  • Focusing on murmur grade rather than congestion signs can delay CHF recognition.
  • Ignoring azotemia risk when using diuretics and ACE inhibitors together.
  • Failing to adjust treatment when electrolyte abnormalities emerge.
  • Omitting client education on resting respiratory rate monitoring at home.
