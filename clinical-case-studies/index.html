How to Approach Case Studies

  1. Signalment and History: Extract risk factors and prior probability from breed, age, sex, and presenting complaint.
  2. Physical Examination: Prioritize abnormal findings and construct a problem list.
  3. Diagnostic Data: Interpret lab work and imaging in context; recognize when data is incomplete.
  4. Differential Diagnosis: Rank possibilities by likelihood and testability; avoid premature closure.
  5. Treatment Planning: Match interventions to diagnosis while addressing immediate patient needs.
  6. Monitoring and Follow-up: Define success criteria and contingency plans.
