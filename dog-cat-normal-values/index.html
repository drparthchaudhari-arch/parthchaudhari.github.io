How to Use Normal Values During Study

  • Confirm the species-specific interval first (dog vs cat differences are meaningful).
  • Interpret abnormalities in context: signalment, hydration status, and trend over time.
  • Link critical abnormalities to stabilization priorities before broad differential expansion.

Species Differences and Variability Warnings

  • Cats can show stress hyperglycemia, so glucose values need context before endocrine conclusions.
  • Breed, age, and body condition can shift baseline expectations, especially for creatinine and liver enzymes.
  • Single values can mislead when fluid shifts or sampling artifacts are present.

When to Escalate Immediately

  • Rapidly rising lactate, severe electrolyte derangement, or worsening acid-base status.
  • Marked anemia with perfusion compromise or active bleeding signs.
  • Respiratory distress, syncope, altered mentation, or arrhythmia alongside abnormal labs.
