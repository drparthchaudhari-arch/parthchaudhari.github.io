Anesthesia and Analgesia

Pre-op risk review with monitoring and multimodal pain-control planning.

Back to Comprehensive Guide Practice Questions

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Pre-anesthetic risk classification and protocol design by patient status and species.
  • Balanced anesthesia planning with induction, maintenance, and rescue drug choices.
  • Intraoperative monitoring interpretation (BP, capnography, oxygenation, temperature).
  • Hypotension, hypoventilation, and delayed recovery troubleshooting.
  • Multimodal analgesia plans for acute and perioperative pain.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • An anesthetized dog becomes hypotensive with acceptable depth. Which corrective step is preferred first?
  • An obese brachycephalic patient requires sedation and intubation. What airway planning priority is most critical?
  • A horse has rough recovery behavior post-anesthesia. Which risk-reduction strategy is most defensible?
  • A cat undergoing orthopedic surgery needs pain control. Which multimodal analgesia concept is highest yield?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: anesthetic monitoring interpretation and rescue actions are heavily tested.
  • Horses: induction/recovery risk and field-anesthesia concerns appear often.
  • Ruminants and swine: practical restraint, sedation, and food-animal constraints may appear.
  • Birds and exotics: thermoregulation and airway management principles are testable.
