What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Herd-level problem solving for respiratory, enteric, mastitis, and metabolic disease clusters.
  • Calf medicine triage: dehydration scoring, colostrum failures, and early mortality prevention.
  • Transition-cow disease links: ketosis, DA, milk fever, and postpartum infection risk.
  • Reproductive performance interpretation using herd data and targeted interventions.
  • Biosecurity, vaccination planning, and treatment-withdrawal compliance.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A feedlot cohort develops fever and cough. Which syndrome and control step are most likely?
  • A fresh cow presents with low appetite and ketone positivity. What metabolic pathway is being tested?
  • A calf with watery diarrhea and sunken eyes arrives. What fluid priority is first?
  • Bulk tank and mastitis records worsen over a month. Which herd-level change should be reviewed first?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dairy and beef cattle are directly tested with both individual and herd-level questions.
  • Calf medicine commonly appears as dehydration and infectious disease triage stems.
  • Reproductive management and transition-cow metrics are frequent NAVLE production blocks.
  • Public-health and residue-awareness often integrate with bovine treatment decisions.
