What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Murmur workups: localization clues, likely lesion sets, and when to prioritize echo versus stabilization.
  • CHF recognition versus noncardiogenic respiratory distress, including rapid staging and treatment logic.
  • Arrhythmia interpretation and first therapeutic move (atrial fibrillation, ventricular ectopy, AV block patterns).
  • Systemic hypertension cases with target-organ risk and recheck scheduling.
  • Feline cardiomyopathy thromboembolism risk counseling and antithrombotic planning.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog with cough, murmur, and resting tachypnea presents. What is the most likely immediate management path?
  • A cat with dyspnea and no cough has pleural effusion. Which cardiology differential rises to the top?
  • Syncope with tachyarrhythmia on ECG is shown. What rhythm is most likely and what is the first action?
  • A hypertensive patient has acute ocular signs. Which target-organ risk changes the urgency of therapy?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs: myxomatous mitral valve disease and DCM pathway questions are high yield.
  • Cats: hypertrophic cardiomyopathy and arterial thromboembolism prevention are frequent.
  • Horses: atrial fibrillation and exercise intolerance scenarios are important.
  • Cattle: traumatic pericarditis and right-sided failure style questions can appear.
