What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • CBC, chemistry, and urinalysis integration into clinical problem lists.
  • Acid-base and electrolyte interpretation under emergency and chronic-disease contexts.
  • Regenerative versus nonregenerative response interpretation and timeline logic.
  • Inflammation patterns and stress leukogram differentiation.
  • Preanalytical artifact recognition (hemolysis, lipemia, delayed sample handling).

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog has high ALP, mild hyperglycemia, and stress leukogram. Which interpretation is most defensible first?
  • Metabolic acidosis with low bicarbonate and high anion gap is identified. Which differential bucket rises?
  • A cat anemia appears nonregenerative on day 2. What timing caveat changes interpretation?
  • Chemistry values do not match clinical status and sample is hemolyzed. What should be done next?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: CBC/chem/UA integration is one of the most frequent NAVLE skill checks.
  • Horses: muscle enzymes, inflammatory markers, and hydration status interpretation can appear.
  • Ruminants: metabolic profile interpretation in transition and production disease is important.
  • Swine and poultry: population-level lab interpretation may appear in outbreak contexts.
