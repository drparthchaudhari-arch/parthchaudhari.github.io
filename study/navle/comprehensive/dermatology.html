Pruritus and skin lesion algorithms for efficient dermatology case progression.

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Pruritus algorithm sequencing: parasites, infection, allergy, and endocrine contributors.
  • Lesion description and cytology-based first diagnostics in skin and ear disease.
  • Otitis externa or media linkage to primary dermatologic disease.
  • Immune-mediated versus infectious dermatosis pattern recognition.
  • Long-term management planning with relapse prevention and client adherence.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A pruritic dog has seasonal flare and recurrent otitis. Which workup sequence is most defensible?
  • A cat has crusting facial lesions and eosinophilic inflammation. Which differential set is highest yield?
  • A beef herd has patchy alopecia and crusting lesions. Which contagious dermatologic concern is prioritized?
  • A dog fails repeated empiric antibiotics for pyoderma. Which diagnostic step is now required?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs: allergic skin disease and secondary infection patterns are very common.
  • Cats: eosinophilic and hypersensitivity patterns are frequently tested.
  • Horses: insect hypersensitivity and dermatophyte differentials can appear.
  • Ruminants and swine: ectoparasites and contagious skin disease have herd-level context.
