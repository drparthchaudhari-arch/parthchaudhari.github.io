What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Best-modality choice based on clinical question, urgency, and patient stability.
  • Thoracic and abdominal pattern recognition for common NAVLE disease presentations.
  • Orthopedic imaging interpretation basics including fracture and joint-space assessment.
  • Artifact versus pathology differentiation and image-quality troubleshooting.
  • Radiation-safety principles and contrast-study indications.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dyspneic patient is unstable. Which imaging step is safest and highest-yield first?
  • Abdominal radiographs are equivocal in suspected obstruction. What modality is preferred next?
  • A lameness case has normal survey films but persistent pain. What imaging escalation is most appropriate?
  • A herd-level respiratory case needs practical diagnostics. Which imaging strategy fits field constraints?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: thoracic and abdominal radiographic interpretation is heavily represented.
  • Horses: lameness-focused imaging and field constraints are common.
  • Cattle and small ruminants: practical ultrasound and focused imaging in production settings can appear.
  • Swine and poultry: population-focused diagnostics may use limited individual imaging.
