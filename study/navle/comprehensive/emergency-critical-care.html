What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Initial triage order: airway, breathing, circulation, perfusion endpoints, and stabilization sequence.
  • Shock type recognition (hypovolemic, distributive, cardiogenic, obstructive) and first-line resuscitation.
  • RECOVER-style CPR decisions, rhythm-based drug selection, and post-ROSC monitoring priorities.
  • Emergency protocols for DKA, sepsis, GDV, and heat injury using timed reassessment checkpoints.
  • Toxicosis decision points: decontamination timing, antidote choice, and organ-support triage.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A collapsed dog with weak pulses and lactate 8 mmol/L arrives. Which immediate intervention comes first?
  • During CPR, the ECG changes rhythm after epinephrine. What is the next algorithm step?
  • A DKA cat's glucose is falling but potassium drops to 2.8 mEq/L. What should be adjusted now?
  • A suspected toxin ingestion happened 90 minutes ago in an asymptomatic patient. What decontamination plan is most appropriate?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: primary NAVLE emergency load, especially triage/CPR/sepsis/toxicosis.
  • Horses: colic, endotoxemia, and field-stabilization logistics are common scenario styles.
  • Cattle: calf scours dehydration, downer-cow triage, and herd-impact emergency decisions can appear.
  • Swine and poultry: outbreak-level emergency response may be tested at population scale.
