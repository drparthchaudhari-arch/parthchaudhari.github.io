What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Diabetes mellitus diagnosis, stabilization, and insulin-monitoring interpretation.
  • Hyperadrenocorticism and hypoadrenocorticism test selection and emergency decision points.
  • Thyroid disease interpretation with species-specific differences in testing and treatment.
  • Calcium and phosphorus endocrine disorders with urgent complication triage.
  • Endocrine comorbidity recognition in chronic, multisystem NAVLE cases.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A PU/PD dog has stress leukogram and elevated ALP. Which endocrine test sequence is most defensible?
  • A weak, bradycardic patient with hyperkalemia arrives. Which endocrine emergency is most likely?
  • A diabetic cat remains hyperglycemic despite insulin adjustments. What common confounder should be checked next?
  • An older horse with laminitis and hirsutism is presented. Which endocrine syndrome best fits?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs: Cushing disease, Addison disease, diabetes, and hypothyroidism dominate.
  • Cats: diabetes and hyperthyroidism are core high-yield patterns.
  • Horses: PPID and equine metabolic syndrome are common testing themes.
  • Cattle: ketosis and hypocalcemia may appear as production endocrinology contexts.
