High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Colic triage and referral threshold decisions under time and resource constraints.
  • Equine respiratory disease workups and outbreak-management considerations.

  • Field emergency stabilization and transport-readiness decisions.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A horse with persistent colic and reflux is worsening. What referral decision is most appropriate now?
  • A stable has febrile respiratory disease spread. Which immediate biosecurity step is mandatory?
  • An older horse has muscle loss and recurrent laminitis. Which endocrine diagnosis should be prioritized?
  • Acute neurologic signs appear in one horse after a recent event movement. Which outbreak concern rises first?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Colic and emergency stabilization are core horse-specific NAVLE themes.
  • Respiratory and infectious control in stable populations are commonly tested.
  • Endocrine-laminitis integration is a frequent chronic-case stem style.
  • Cardiorespiratory and exercise-intolerance cases can blend internal medicine with performance medicine.
