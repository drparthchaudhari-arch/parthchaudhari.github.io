Gastroenterology and Hepatology

Vomiting, diarrhea, liver disease, and pancreatitis pathways for rapid case narrowing.

Back to Comprehensive Guide Practice Questions

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Vomiting and diarrhea triage with dehydration/perfusion severity and obstruction risk stratification.
  • Acute abdomen differentiation: pancreatitis, obstruction, peritonitis, and GI foreign body pathways.
  • Liver enzyme patterns (hepatocellular versus cholestatic) and next-step diagnostics.
  • Acid-base and electrolyte disturbances in severe GI disease.
  • Protein-losing enteropathy and chronic enteropathy long-form case workups.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A vomiting dog has painful cranial abdomen and elevated lipase. What diagnosis pathway is most likely?
  • A cat with icterus has mixed liver enzyme changes. What is the next best diagnostic step?
  • Acute diarrhea in calves spreads through a group. What herd-level intervention is prioritized first?
  • A horse with colic and reflux is worsening despite fluids. What escalation is most appropriate?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: pancreatitis, chronic enteropathy, hepatobiliary disease, and obstruction are common.
  • Horses: colic localization and surgical versus medical threshold decisions are frequent.
  • Cattle: displaced abomasum, ruminal disorders, and calf enteric disease can be tested.
  • Small ruminants: enterotoxemia and parasitic GI disease patterns may appear.
