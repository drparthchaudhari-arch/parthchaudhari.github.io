Aquatics

ICVA scope centers on multisystemic disease with heavy water-quality, husbandry, infectious, and toxicity emphasis.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Water chemistry failures: ammonia, nitrite, nitrate, pH, dissolved oxygen, salinity, and gas supersaturation.
  • Infectious categories: bacterial, protozoal, fungal or water mold, and selected viral syndromes.
  • Parasite patterns: internal and external infestations tied to stocking and filtration issues.
  • Toxic and trauma contexts: chlorine or chloramine, metal toxicosis, and handling-related injury.

How stems are often asked

  • Aquatic system crash scenario where chemistry values point to the primary correction step.
  • Tank-level outbreak stem asking which diagnosis bucket and first control action should be prioritized.

Pair with VetLudics topics

  • Infectious Disease
  • Parasitology
  • Toxicology
  • Nutrition and Metabolism

Bird

ICVA bird coverage spans endocrine, GI, integument, respiratory, reproductive, behavior, infectious, and multisystemic disease patterns.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Respiratory and air sac disease with concurrent infectious differentials.
  • Nutritional and metabolic conditions such as hypocalcemia, gout, and hypovitaminosis patterns.
  • Reproductive and behavior-linked conditions including egg-associated disease and chronic behavior problems.
  • Zoonotic and reportable infection concerns in flock or aviary settings.

How stems are often asked

  • Single-bird respiratory decline with husbandry details that identify the most likely category.
  • Aviary outbreak stem asking for immediate containment and test-first strategy.

Bovine

ICVA bovine scope is broad: cardiovascular through multisystemic disease, including herd-level infectious, metabolic, reproductive, and food-animal safety contexts.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Production GI and metabolic disease: abomasal disorders, rumen or acid-base disruptions, and ketosis/milk fever patterns.
  • Respiratory complexes and calf disease syndromes tied to management and housing risk.
  • Reproductive and periparturient disease: dystocia, retained placenta, metritis, infertility, and mastitis-endotoxemia pathways.
  • Multisystemic herd threats including clostridial, toxic, parasitic, and reportable disease concerns.

How stems are often asked

  • Dairy or feedlot scenario where herd data and one key exam finding determine first intervention.
  • Food-animal case asking for treatment plus residue-safe compliance decision.

  • Bovine Medicine
  • Reproduction and Theriogenology

Camelidae and Cervidae

ICVA places camelid and cervid questions across cardiovascular, GI, integument, musculoskeletal, neurologic, reproductive, respiratory, behavior, and multisystemic categories.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • GI and nutrition-linked disease including enteritis, lipidosis, parasitism, and grain-related events.
  • Neurologic and multisystemic conditions with infectious and toxic differentials.
  • Musculoskeletal and dermatologic conditions tied to trace minerals, husbandry, and foot-health challenges.
  • Reproductive complications including infertility, abortion pathways, and dystocia decisions.

How stems are often asked

  • Field-case triage stem in alpaca or deer where limited diagnostics require best-first action.
  • Herd reproductive or neonatal loss scenario asking for top differential category and next test step.

Canine

ICVA canine coverage is full-spectrum across organ systems and emergencies, including internal medicine, surgery, behavior, special senses, and multisystemic disease.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Emergency and critical patterns: shock, arrest, toxic exposure, DKA, and sepsis pathways.
  • Cardiorespiratory and endocrine disease: CHF, arrhythmia, airway disease, diabetes, adrenal and thyroid disorders.
  • GI, neuro, urinary, and reproductive problems requiring algorithmic triage and recheck planning.
  • Dermatology and ophthalmology through common high-yield differentials and first-line workups.

How stems are often asked

  • Classic signalment plus 3 to 4 findings with one critical next-step management decision.
  • Progression or complication stem asking what changed in diagnosis stage or treatment priority.

Equine

ICVA equine questions span colic, lameness, respiratory, reproductive, neurologic, ophthalmic, and multisystemic disease, often in field-decision contexts.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Colic and GI emergency pathways, including medical versus surgical threshold decisions.
  • Lameness and musculoskeletal performance-limiting disease with imaging and localization logic.
  • Respiratory and infectious disease plus stable-level control and biosecurity choices.
  • Reproductive, neonatal, and periparturient syndromes with time-sensitive interventions.

How stems are often asked

  • Field emergency with constrained diagnostics where referral timing is the key tested decision.
  • Stable-level infectious or respiratory case asking for first containment and diagnostic sequence.

Feline

ICVA feline coverage mirrors canine breadth but emphasizes cat-specific cardiomyopathy, endocrine, urinary, respiratory, behavior, and infectious patterns.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Cardio-thromboembolic and hypertensive disease with emergency dyspnea or blindness stems.
  • Endocrine and metabolic disease including diabetes, thyroid, and adrenal-related presentations.
  • Lower urinary and renal disease: idiopathic cystitis, obstruction, CKD or AKI, and electrolyte consequences.
  • Multisystemic infectious and toxic syndromes, especially in indoor-outdoor or multi-cat contexts.

How stems are often asked

  • Minimal-sign cat that suddenly decompensates, testing recognition of hidden high-risk disease.
  • Recurring clinical pattern stem where best monitoring or relapse-prevention step is tested.

Other Small Mammal

ICVA includes chinchillas, ferrets, guinea pigs, hamsters, primates, rabbits, and rats or mice with strong husbandry-linked disease emphasis.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Species-specific GI and nutrition disorders tied to dental disease, dysbiosis, and husbandry mismatch.
  • Heat stress, respiratory disease, and external parasite burden in high-density or poor-environment setups.
  • Ferret and rabbit endocrine, neoplastic, and infectious patterns that require species-aware differential narrowing.
  • Zoonotic and high-consequence infection awareness in select primate and rodent contexts.

How stems are often asked

  • Exotic species case where husbandry details reveal the primary disease driver.
  • Small-mammal emergency stem that tests stabilization priority before definitive diagnosis.

Ovine and Caprine

ICVA sheep and goat content emphasizes GI, respiratory, neurologic, integument, musculoskeletal, and urinary disease with flock-level management implications.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Enteric and metabolic disease including bloat, enterotoxemia, and rumen-related disruption.
  • Parasite burden, foot disease, and lameness clusters with productivity impact.
  • Neurologic and respiratory disease patterns requiring fast infectious versus toxic differentiation.
  • Male urinary obstruction and mineral-balance risks in intensive management systems.

How stems are often asked

  • Flock-level morbidity stem asking for highest-yield immediate control action.
  • Single recumbent small-ruminant stem testing emergency differential hierarchy and first intervention.

Porcine

ICVA porcine coverage focuses on herd respiratory and GI disease, neurologic and toxic events, behavior problems, and high-consequence regulatory disease awareness.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Enteric outbreaks in piglets or growers with rapid spread and management-linked risk factors.
  • Respiratory complex disease with mixed bacterial or viral contributors and production losses.
  • Neurologic and toxic clusters including salt or water-balance disorders and feed-linked toxicosis.
  • Regulatory and transboundary disease scenarios requiring immediate reporting logic.

How stems are often asked

  • Barn-level disease trend question testing first containment and sampling strategy.
  • Food-animal therapy stem that combines efficacy, stewardship, and compliance decisions.

Poultry

Reptile

ICVA reptile coverage is multisystemic, with species groups such as chelonians, lizards, and snakes and strong husbandry-linked disease representation.

Likely NAVLE diagnosis clusters

  • Husbandry-driven disease: thermal, UV, environmental, and nutrition mismatch problems.
  • Respiratory, stomatitis, and septic processes in immunocompromised husbandry conditions.
  • Dystocia, prolapse, and urinary or cloacal disorders requiring urgent stabilization.
  • Parasitic, dermatologic, and shedding-related disorders with species-specific handling decisions.

How stems are often asked

  • Single reptile case where enclosure parameters reveal the underlying diagnosis pathway.
  • Mixed-sign emergency stem testing first stabilization action before definitive workup.
