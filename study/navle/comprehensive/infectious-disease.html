Infectious Disease

Systematic infectious workups with isolation and treatment escalation decisions.

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Fever-of-unknown-origin style workups using exposure history and system-based sampling.
  • Isolation and biosecurity decisions in contagious disease scenarios.
  • PCR, serology, culture, and paired-sample interpretation for test selection questions.
  • Zoonotic risk communication and client/public-health guidance responsibilities.
  • Empiric versus targeted antimicrobial strategy with stewardship rationale.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A shelter outbreak with fever and respiratory signs is identified. Which containment step is first?
  • A tick-exposed dog has thrombocytopenia and lethargy. What test combination is most useful initially?
  • A dairy herd has abortion storm concerns. Which sample strategy best supports diagnosis?
  • A zoonotic suspect is identified in clinic. What communication and reporting action is most appropriate?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: respiratory, vector-borne, and shelter-medicine infection patterns are frequent.
  • Cattle: respiratory and reproductive outbreak investigations are commonly tested.
  • Horses: respiratory and neurologic infectious disease differentials can appear.
  • Small ruminants, swine, and poultry: herd or flock biosecurity decision-making is high yield.
