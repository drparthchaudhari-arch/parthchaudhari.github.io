What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • AKI versus CKD differentiation and immediate fluid and monitoring priorities.
  • Urinary obstruction and post-obstructive care, including electrolyte danger points.
  • Proteinuria and urine concentrating ability interpretation in chronic cases.
  • Acid-base and electrolyte interpretation in renal disease progression.
  • UTI, pyelonephritis, and urolith management with recurrence prevention.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • An azotemic cat is dehydrated with isosthenuria. What data best supports AKI versus CKD first?
  • A blocked male cat is unblocked but remains bradycardic. Which electrolyte concern is primary?
  • Persistent proteinuria with stable creatinine is found. What next diagnostic step is most appropriate?
  • A goat with dysuria and crystaluria presents. Which urologic emergency is high on the list?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: urethral obstruction, AKI causes, CKD staging, and proteinuria are core.
  • Goats and sheep: obstructive urolithiasis in males is a classic exam pattern.
  • Cattle: post-renal azotemia and urinary rupture differentials can appear.
  • Horses: pigment nephropathy and renal compromise in critical illness may be tested.
