What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Neuroanatomic localization from gait, mentation, cranial nerve, and postural reaction findings.
  • Seizure first-line stabilization and maintenance-therapy decisions.
  • Vestibular syndrome differentiation: peripheral versus central clues and urgency triggers.
  • Spinal cord injury and disc disease triage with surgery referral thresholds.
  • Neuromuscular disease differentials, including metabolic and toxic mimics.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog is non-ambulatory paraparetic with absent deep pain. What is the urgency tier and likely lesion region?
  • A cat has acute head tilt and nystagmus but normal mentation. Which localization is most likely?
  • Cluster seizures persist after first-line therapy. What escalation is recommended next?
  • A horse with ataxia and cranial deficits is presented. Which central differential bucket is prioritized?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: seizures, vestibular disease, and thoracolumbar disc cases are frequent.
  • Horses: cervical myelopathy, EPM-style localization, and trauma are important.
  • Ruminants: listeriosis, polioencephalomalacia, and metabolic differentials can appear.
  • Swine and poultry: neuro-outbreak pattern recognition may appear in population blocks.
