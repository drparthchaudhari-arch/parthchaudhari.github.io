Nutrition and Metabolism

Energy requirement planning and therapeutic nutrition decisions for recovery and chronic care.

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Energy requirement calculations and feeding plans by life stage and disease state.
  • Body-condition and muscle-condition scoring interpretation in treatment plans.
  • Therapeutic diet selection for renal, hepatic, GI, and endocrine disorders.
  • Metabolic disease nutrition management across companion and production species.
  • Refeeding and cachexia-risk planning in prolonged illness.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • An obese diabetic cat is starting insulin. Which nutrition strategy best supports glycemic control?
  • A CKD dog has poor appetite and weight loss. What diet priority is most important?
  • A transition dairy cow group develops metabolic problems. What nutrition risk factor is most likely?
  • A horse with laminitis history needs diet planning. Which feeding change has highest priority?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: obesity, chronic-disease diets, and calorie planning are common exam themes.
  • Horses: laminitis and metabolic-syndrome diet control are high yield.
  • Cattle: transition-cow nutrition and ketosis prevention can be tested.
  • Small ruminants: pregnancy toxemia and energy-balance decisions are important.
