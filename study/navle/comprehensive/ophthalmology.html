What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Painful red-eye triage: ulcer, uveitis, glaucoma, and immediate test sequence.
  • Vision-loss localization using menace/PLR findings and fundic exam priorities.
  • Corneal ulcer depth, infection risk, and urgent referral triggers.
  • Eyelid and conjunctival disease pattern recognition and treatment logic.
  • Species-specific ocular emergency recognition in both companion and food/horse species.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • Dog with blepharospasm, epiphora, and a fluorescein-positive lesion: what is the first-line management priority?
  • Cat with acute blindness and systemic hypertension signs: which ocular lesion is most likely?
  • Horse with recurrent painful eye episodes and miosis: what diagnosis should be prioritized?
  • Calf group with tearing, photophobia, and corneal opacity: which herd-level eye disease is most likely?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs: keratoconjunctivitis sicca, corneal ulcers, glaucoma, and lens-related emergencies are high yield.
  • Cats: herpetic keratitis, uveitis, and hypertensive retinopathy with retinal detachment are common stem styles.
  • Horses: equine recurrent uveitis and corneal trauma or ulcer questions are important.
  • Cattle: infectious bovine keratoconjunctivitis (pinkeye) and control strategy are tested.
  • Sheep and goats: infectious keratoconjunctivitis and trauma differentials can appear.
  • Birds and exotics: clinically important, but free high-yield source packet is still queued.
