Lameness localization and orthopedic decision trees for trauma and chronic disease.

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Lameness localization and gait interpretation across forelimb, hindlimb, and axial pain patterns.
  • Fracture stabilization priorities and imaging-guided orthopedic planning.
  • Cruciate disease, luxations, and tendon or ligament injury differentials.
  • Joint disease workups including septic arthritis versus immune-mediated processes.
  • Rehabilitation and return-to-function decisions after orthopedic intervention.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog has acute non-weight-bearing hindlimb lameness after activity. Which orthopedic differential is highest?
  • A horse shows chronic forelimb lameness that worsens on hard ground. What localization concept is being tested?
  • A calf has swollen painful joint with fever. What type of arthropathy should be prioritized?
  • Radiographs show fracture displacement near a growth plate. What management principle is most important first?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: cruciate disease, patellar luxation, fractures, and joint disease are common.
  • Horses: lameness localization and performance-limiting orthopedic lesions are high yield.
  • Cattle: infectious and traumatic lameness with hoof or joint involvement are tested.
  • Small ruminants: foot disorders and trauma may appear in herd-health contexts.
