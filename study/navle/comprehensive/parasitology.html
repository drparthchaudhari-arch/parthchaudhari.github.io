What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Life-cycle based parasite control planning and strategic deworming logic.
  • Fecal diagnostics interpretation (flotation, sedimentation, quantitative egg counts).
  • Endo- and ectoparasite treatment selection by species, age, and production setting.
  • Anthelmintic resistance recognition and resistance-mitigation planning.
  • Vector-borne disease prevention counseling for small-animal practice.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A young dog has intermittent GI signs and negative single fecal flotation. What is the best next step?
  • High fecal egg counts persist in a sheep flock after treatment. What management issue is most likely?
  • A cat in a mosquito-dense area lacks prevention. Which parasite prevention message is highest priority?
  • A horse herd deworms on fixed calendar intervals only. What resistance-risk concept is being tested?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: heartworm and intestinal parasite prevention questions are common.
  • Horses: strongyle control and resistance management are high yield.
  • Sheep and goats: Haemonchus-driven anemia and selective treatment frameworks are frequent.
  • Swine and poultry: production-cycle parasite control can appear in population medicine sections.
