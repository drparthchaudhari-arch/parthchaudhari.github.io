What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Mechanism-based drug selection under species-specific contraindications.
  • Dose interval and route decisions using PK/PD reasoning in exam-style case prompts.
  • Adverse-effect recognition and rational therapy adjustment.
  • Drug interactions and monitoring plans for multi-drug cases.
  • Extra-label use constraints and antimicrobial stewardship in food and companion animals.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A patient on multiple chronic drugs develops new GI and neurologic signs. Which pharmacology issue is most likely?
  • A food-animal treatment is planned with no labeled option. Which legal framework controls next steps?
  • A culture result returns after empiric therapy started. What stewardship-focused change is best?
  • A species-specific contraindication is present for a common drug. What safer class should be considered first?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • All NAVLE species appear in pharmacology questions, often through contraindication traps.
  • Dogs and cats: adverse-effect and interaction questions are frequent.
  • Food animals: extra-label and residue-compliance logic is very high yield.
  • Equine and exotic cases may test route practicality and withdrawal or competition constraints.
