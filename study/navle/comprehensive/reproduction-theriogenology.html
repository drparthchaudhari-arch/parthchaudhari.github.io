What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Estrous cycle timing and breeding management decisions across species.
  • Infertility and subfertility workups including male and female factors.
  • Pregnancy diagnosis timing, interpretation limits, and follow-up plans.
  • Dystocia triage and when to shift from medical to surgical management.
  • Neonatal viability, colostrum management, and early-life risk reduction.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A bitch misses expected conception after timed breeding. Which diagnostic step is best first?
  • A mare has prolonged gestation with udder changes. What monitoring decision is most appropriate?
  • A ewe in labor is exhausted with malpresentation suspected. What is the next intervention?
  • A dairy herd has declining conception rates. Which reproductive metric should be reviewed first?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: timing, fertility workups, and dystocia management are common.
  • Horses: breeding-season management and placental or foaling risk scenarios are high yield.
  • Cattle: herd fertility metrics and postpartum reproductive disease frequently appear.
  • Sheep, goats, and camelids: pregnancy management and neonatal survival concepts are important.
