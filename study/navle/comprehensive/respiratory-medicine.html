Respiratory Medicine

Upper and lower airway diagnostics with oxygenation-first emergency handling.

Back to Comprehensive Guide Practice Questions

What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Upper versus lower airway localization from history, auscultation, and response to oxygen/minimal handling.
  • Pleural space disease versus pulmonary parenchymal disease differentiation.
  • Diagnostic sequencing for coughing, dyspnea, stridor, and cyanosis in unstable patients.
  • Infectious respiratory disease workups with isolation and herd or household implications.
  • Ventilation strategy basics and monitoring endpoints in critical respiratory compromise.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog with inspiratory stridor and stress-related collapse presents. What airway localization is most likely?
  • Thoracic imaging shows pleural fluid in a dyspneic cat. Which immediate procedure is prioritized?
  • A feedlot outbreak with fever and cough appears. Which respiratory differential pattern best fits first?
  • A horse has exercise intolerance and abnormal noise. What upper-airway condition is most likely?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: upper-airway obstruction, pneumonia, and pleural-space emergencies dominate.
  • Horses: upper-airway performance disease and infectious respiratory outbreaks are emphasized.
  • Cattle: bovine respiratory disease complex scenarios are common in production medicine blocks.
  • Small ruminants: parasitic and bacterial pneumonia patterns may be tested.
