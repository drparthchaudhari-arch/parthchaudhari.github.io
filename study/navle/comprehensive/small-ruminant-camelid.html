What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Parasite-associated anemia and strategic treatment planning at flock or herd level.
  • Metabolic disease triage, including pregnancy toxemia and urolithiasis risk.
  • Respiratory and enteric outbreak evaluation with practical field diagnostics.
  • Neonatal management and dam-health integration in lamb and kid survival.
  • Preventive medicine scheduling with nutrition and reproduction crossover.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A goat herd has pale mucous membranes and poor weight gain. Which disease process is most likely?
  • A late-gestation ewe is recumbent and ketotic. What metabolic disorder is highest priority?
  • A male llama has stranguria and abdominal discomfort. Which emergency differential should be prioritized?
  • A kid outbreak with diarrhea appears after weather stress. What first herd-level response is most useful?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Sheep and goats: parasite burden, metabolic disease, and reproduction are central exam themes.
  • Camelids: urinary and nutritional disorders can appear in focused species stems.
  • Neonatal disease in lambs and kids is a frequent high-yield production style.
  • Public-health and antimicrobial-use decisions can be integrated in these species blocks.
