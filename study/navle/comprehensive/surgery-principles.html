What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Asepsis and operating-room flow decisions that reduce contamination risk.
  • Wound classification, closure strategy, and drainage planning.
  • Perioperative antimicrobial timing and stewardship-compliant use.
  • Surgical complication recognition (dehiscence, infection, hemorrhage) and response.
  • Postoperative monitoring and discharge instruction priorities.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A contaminated wound is presented for closure. Which closure strategy is most defensible?
  • Post-op patient has increasing pain and tachycardia with pale mucous membranes. What complication is highest priority?
  • A surgery case has prolonged procedure time and tissue trauma. How should contamination risk influence plan?
  • A farm-animal surgery case requires residue-aware planning. Which principle must be included?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: wound management and perioperative complications are central.
  • Horses: surgical referral timing and post-op colic or incisional issues are important.
  • Food animals: withdrawal-time and residue considerations can be integrated.
  • Exotics: tissue fragility and recovery planning may appear as principle questions.
