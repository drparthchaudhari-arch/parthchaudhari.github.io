What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Group-level disease investigations in respiratory and enteric production outbreaks.
  • Biosecurity, vaccination, and movement-control planning under farm constraints.
  • Necropsy-and-history based differential building for herd/flock events.
  • Feed, environment, and management contributors to morbidity spikes.
  • Residue and food-safety considerations in treatment planning.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A swine unit develops progressive respiratory signs in growers. Which syndrome and control move are likely first?
  • A poultry flock has sudden neurologic and mortality increase. Which immediate investigation priority applies?
  • A farm modifies feed source before disease spike. What epidemiologic clue is being tested?
  • Population treatment is considered in food animals. Which regulatory concept must guide drug selection?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Swine: respiratory complexes and management-linked disease patterns are core.
  • Poultry: flock-level toxic, infectious, and environmental differential questions are common.
  • Food-animal residue and withdrawal principles frequently pair with treatment questions.
  • Outbreak control logic is tested more than single-patient intensive care in these species.
