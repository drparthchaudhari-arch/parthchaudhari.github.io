What NAVLE Can Ask

High-Yield Prompt Map

  • Exposure-risk calculations from dose, concentration, species sensitivity, and time since ingestion.
  • Decontamination decisions: emesis, activated charcoal, dermal decontamination, and contraindications.
  • Antidote selection and supportive-care prioritization by toxin class.
  • Serial monitoring strategy for renal, hepatic, neurologic, and cardiac complications.
  • Client communication for household and farm safety after toxic exposures.

How NAVLE Usually Asks (One-Liners)

  • A dog ingested dark chocolate two hours ago and is asymptomatic. What is the best immediate plan?
  • A cat presents with tremors after unknown ingestion. What stabilization step precedes full toxin identification?
  • A horse in a mixed-feed setting develops acute weakness. Which tox history detail is most critical first?
  • A toxin-exposed calf appears normal initially. Which delayed complication monitoring plan is most appropriate?

Species-Focused Question Styles

  • Dogs and cats: household toxicants and medication exposures are high-frequency exam patterns.
  • Horses: feed contamination and pasture-related toxicosis questions can appear.
  • Cattle and small ruminants: plant and chemical exposure with herd implications are tested.
  • Birds: heavy-metal and inhalational toxicity can appear in species-specific stems.
