Clickable CHF Algorithm

Step 1: Stabilize respiratory distress

  • Minimal handling and oxygen support first.
  • Obtain focused thoracic POCUS/radiographs once stable.
  • If severe distress with crackles: treat presumptively for cardiogenic edema.

Step 2: Confirm likely CHF pattern

  • Dog: cough + murmur + tachypnea/resting RR increase.
  • Cat: open-mouth breathing, pleural effusion or pulmonary edema signs.
  • Integrate BNP/echo findings when available.

Step 3: Decongest

  • Furosemide titrated to respiratory response and perfusion.
  • Consider vasodilator support where indicated.
  • Monitor hydration, electrolytes, and renal values.

Step 4: Outpatient maintenance plan

  • Core regimen often includes loop diuretic + pimobendan (dogs) +/- ACE inhibitor.
  • Owner education: resting respiratory rate trend is mandatory.
  • Adjust sodium intake and exercise based on stage.

Step 5: Recheck and escalation

  • Recheck in 3-7 days after stabilization, then stage-based intervals.
  • Worsening tachypnea or syncope triggers immediate reassessment.
  • Escalate diuresis and diagnostics if refractory signs persist.

Pattern Cards

Dog + cough + murmur

Think chronic valvular disease with CHF progression until proven otherwise.

Cat + dyspnea + no cough

Prioritize feline cardiomyopathy and pleural space evaluation quickly.

Ascites + jugular distention

Right-sided CHF/pericardial disease rises on differential list.

Tachypnea after diuretic reduction

Likely recongestion; reassess volume status and cardiopulmonary imaging.
