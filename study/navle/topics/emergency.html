GDV Protocol

  1. Triage: Identify non-productive retching, distended abdomen, shock signs.
  2. Immediate stabilization: Two large-bore IV catheters, isotonic shock doses in aliquots.
  3. Decompression: Orogastric tube if possible; trocarization if unstable.
  4. Diagnostics while stabilizing: Lactate trend, right lateral radiographs if feasible.
  5. Surgery: Derotation, gastric viability check, splenic assessment, gastropexy.
  6. Post-op watch: Ventricular arrhythmias, reperfusion injury, perfusion endpoints.

DKA Algorithm

1. Fluids first

Start isotonic crystalloids before insulin; restore perfusion and correct dehydration.

2. Electrolyte plan

Expect potassium and phosphorus decline with insulin and rehydration.

3. Regular insulin CRI or IM protocol

Start only after initial fluids and potassium strategy are in place.

4. Monitor every 2-4 hours

Glucose, electrolytes, acid-base, mentation, and urine output drive adjustments.

5. Treat precipitating cause

Pancreatitis, infection, endocrine disease, or non-compliance often trigger DKA.

Toxicity Triage

Known toxin + high-risk species

Treat as emergency even before full tox dose calculation.

Symptomatic patient

Prioritize airway/perfusion/seizure control and then decontamination decisions.

Unknown dose ingestion

Conservative approach: assume potentially toxic until proven otherwise.

Delay > 6 hours

Focus on complication monitoring and organ-supportive care pathways.
