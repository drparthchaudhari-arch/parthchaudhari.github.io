2.1 Bovine

Cardiovascular

  • Atrial fibrillation is the most common bovine arrhythmia.
  • Congestive heart failure causes: pericarditis, endocarditis, bovine high mountain disease, cor pulmonale.
  • Bovine high mountain disease: hypoxia at altitude causes pulmonary hypertension and ventral edema; move cattle to lower elevation.
  • TEME (Histophilus somnus): hemorrhagic meningoencephalitis/abscess patterns.

Gastrointestinal

  • LDA: left ping in the 9th to 13th rib region, common in postpartum dairy cows.
  • RDA/abomasal volvulus: life-threatening, aggressive fluid resuscitation and surgery.
  • Hardware disease (TRP): arched back, positive grunt/withers/pole tests, fever, milk drop; treat with magnet and antibiotics or rumenotomy.
  • Vagal indigestion: apple-pear abdomen, L-shaped rumen, mushy rumen contents, rightward rumen extension.
  • Calf enteritis causes: rotavirus, coronavirus, ETEC, Salmonella, Clostridium perfringens, coccidia, Cryptosporidium.
  • Winter dysentery in adults, frothy/free gas bloat, hemorrhagic bowel syndrome, cecal dilation/torsion, salmonellosis, rectal tears (grades I to IV).

Hepatobiliary and Hemic

  • Liver abscesses can progress to vena caval thrombosis.
  • Johne disease: chronic diarrhea, weight loss, bottle jaw; diagnose with ELISA or fecal PCR.
  • Anaplasmosis: extravascular hemolysis, fever, icterus; treat oxytetracycline.
  • BLV lymphoma and babesiosis are key hemic differentials.

Integumentary and Musculoskeletal

  • Digital dermatitis (Treponema/Fusobacterium), footrot (Fusobacterium with Dichelobacter), dermatophilosis, papillomatosis, cancer eye (SCC).
  • Lumpy jaw (Actinomyces bovis), white muscle disease, subsolar abscess, stifle injuries, interdigital fibroma.
  • External parasite focus: lice, ticks, flies, grubs.

Nervous

  • Listeriosis: circling, head tilt, CN deficits, silage pH risk; high-dose penicillin early.
  • PEM: thiamine deficiency/sulfur/lead patterns with blindness and seizures; treat thiamine IV.
  • Tetanus, rabies, obturator/radial nerve paralysis, hypomagnesemic tetany.

Respiratory

  • BRDC complex: Mannheimia, Pasteurella, Histophilus, Mycoplasma plus IBR/BRSV/PI-3.
  • IBR, aspiration/enzootic calf pneumonia, tuberculosis, vena caval thrombosis, necrotic laryngitis.

Special Senses, Urinary, Reproductive

  • Pinkeye (Moraxella bovis): UV worsens; fly control and shade matter.
  • Urinary priorities: urolithiasis, pyelonephritis, ethylene glycol toxicity.
  • Dystocia causes: mismatch, uterine torsion, malposition; 300-day torsion often needs C-section.
  • Retained placenta management, metritis/pyometra complex, milk fever treatment/prevention, pregnancy toxemia induction timing.
  • Abortion panel: IBR, BVD, leptospirosis, Neospora, Brucella, Campylobacter, Tritrichomonas.
  • Mastitis panel: contagious pathogens (Staph aureus, Strep agalactiae) and environmental pathogens (E coli, Klebsiella, Strep uberis).
  • Prolapse emergencies: vaginal prolapse (Buhner) and uterine prolapse replacement with systemic support.

Multisystemic and Reportables

  • BVD acute and mucosal disease, PI control with ear notch PCR and culling.
  • Clostridial complexes: enterotoxemia, blackleg, malignant edema; 7-way/8-way vaccination.
  • Ketosis/acetonemia, laminitis, leptospirosis, neonatal septicemia/FPT, lead toxicity, plant/mycotoxin risks, ionophore toxicity.
  • Salt toxicity/water deprivation and urea poisoning differentials.
  • Anthrax, brucellosis, vesicular stomatitis, FMD/BSE differential awareness and reporting obligations.

2.2 Equine

Cardiovascular and Endocrine

  • Atrial fibrillation: irregularly irregular, no P waves, quinidine or cardioversion; avoid quinidine with CHF.
  • Aortic rupture and ionophore cardiotoxicity are high-severity causes of collapse/death.
  • Valvular disease: mitral regurgitation common.
  • PPID (Cushing): hirsutism, laminitis, muscle wasting, PU/PD; pergolide treatment.
  • EMS: obesity/regional adiposity, hyperinsulinemia, laminitis risk.

Gastrointestinal and Colic

  • Colic surgical indicators: HR over 60, unrelenting pain, reflux over 2 L, distended loops on rectal exam.
  • Large colon impaction, large colon volvulus, nephrosplenic entrapment, epiploic foramen entrapment, intussusception, small intestinal volvulus.
  • Sand colic and enterolith risk patterns (regional and breed factors).
  • Gastric ulceration: bruxism, poor appetite/performance; diagnose by gastroscopy; treat omeprazole/sucralfate.
  • Anterior enteritis (duodenitis/proximal jejunitis): large reflux and endotoxemia.
  • Choke: sedation, NG lavage, aspiration-pneumonia prevention.
  • Acute colitis causes: Salmonella, Clostridium, Potomac horse fever, NSAID toxicity.
  • Lawsonia in weanlings: hypoproteinemia and thickened ileum.
  • Internal parasites: small strongyles, ascarids, tapeworms, pinworms and strategic deworming.

Hemic/Lymphatic and Integumentary

  • EIA (Coggins), EVA, FPT, neonatal isoerythrolysis, CID in Arabians, lymphoma, pigeon fever.
  • Purpura hemorrhagica after strangles exposure/vaccination: edema/petechiae with steroid-centric management.
  • Strangles complications: guttural pouch empyema, purpura hemorrhagica, bastard strangles.
  • Dermatophilosis, dermatophytosis, habronemiasis, sarcoids, melanoma, SCC, photosensitization, aural plaques.

Musculoskeletal

  • Laminitis: P3 rotation/sinking, founder stance, cryotherapy and support protocols.
  • Navicular disease, subsolar abscess, thrush, fracture categories (P3, sesamoid, slab, long bone).
  • DJD spectrum: bog spavin, bone spavin, ringbone.
  • Tendon/ligament injury set: bowed tendon, suspensory desmitis, DDFT injury, rupture syndromes.
  • Septic arthritis/tenosynovitis requires rapid lavage and antibiotics.
  • DOD set: OCD, physitis, subchondral cysts, angular/flexural deformities.
  • Tying-up syndromes: sporadic, RER, PSSM; CK/AST and myoglobinuria interpretation.

Nervous

  • Wobbler syndrome, EPM, EHV-1 myeloencephalopathy, WNV, EEE/WEE/VEE, rabies, tetanus, botulism.
  • Peripheral neuropathies: facial, radial, suprascapular (Sweeney).
  • Vestibular disease, seizures, temporohyoid osteopathy, neonatal maladjustment syndrome.

Respiratory

  • Bacterial pneumonia, Rhodococcus equi pneumonia in foals, pleuropneumonia.
  • RAO/heaves management, EIPH, laryngeal hemiplegia, DDSP, epiglottic entrapment.
  • Guttural pouch disease set: empyema, mycosis (carotid erosion risk), tympany.
  • Sinusitis, ethmoid hematoma, pneumothorax, influenza, EHV respiratory disease.

Ophthalmology, Urinary, Reproductive, Behavior, Multisystemic

  • Corneal ulcer and ERU (moon blindness) treatment rules including no steroids with ulceration.
  • AKI/CKD patterns, ruptured bladder in foals, patent urachus, calcium carbonate urolithiasis.
  • Dystocia management timeline, retained placenta over 3 hours as emergency, placentitis, EHV/EVA abortion clues.
  • Granulosa-theca tumor, cryptorchidism testing logic, infertility workups, twinning reduction.
  • Neonatal septicemia and omphalophlebitis protocols.
  • Cribbing/weaving behavior recognition.
  • Endotoxemia, hyperlipemia, DIC, peritonitis, insect toxicities, anhidrosis, drug toxicities.

2.3 Small Animal - Canine

Cardiovascular

  • Arrhythmia set: AFib, VPC, VTach, third-degree AV block.
  • DCM in large breeds; CHF left vs right sided pattern interpretation.
  • Heartworm disease diagnostics and staged treatment (melarsomine, doxycycline, macrocyclic lactone, strict rest).
  • Systemic hypertension secondary causes and amlodipine use.
  • PDA, pericardial effusion/tamponade, degenerative mitral valve disease.

Endocrine

  • Diabetes mellitus fundamentals and insulin management.
  • DKA protocol: isotonic fluids, regular insulin, potassium/phosphorus repletion, trigger treatment.
  • Cushing disease (pituitary/adrenal) diagnostics and trilostane/mitotane framework.
  • Addison disease: hyponatremia/hyperkalemia, ACTH stimulation confirmation, crisis protocol, chronic DOCP plus prednisone.
  • Hypothyroidism: low T4 plus high TSH and replacement principles.

Gastrointestinal

  • Acute gastroenteritis, HGE/AHDS, parvovirus, pancreatitis, EPI, IBD, foreign body obstruction.
  • GDV emergency sequence including decompression, surgery, and post-op arrhythmia risk.
  • PSS diagnostics (bile acids/ammonia/ultrasound) and medical/surgical options.
  • Biliary mucocele, cirrhosis, constipation, dietary indiscretion, esophageal foreign body/esophagitis, gastric ulcers.
  • Periodontal disease, salivary mucocele, rectal prolapse pathways.

Hemic/Lymphatic

  • Anemia interpretation: regenerative vs non-regenerative.
  • IMHA (spherocytes/agglutination/Coombs), ITP, DIC, rodenticide coagulopathy.
  • Hemangiosarcoma, lymphoma/leukemia, mast cell tumor, SLE, juvenile cellulitis.

Integumentary

  • Pyoderma, atopy, flea allergy dermatitis, food allergy, seborrhea.
  • Dermatophytosis, demodicosis, sarcoptic mange, otitis externa/media, aural hematoma.
  • Bite wounds, acral lick granuloma, anal sac disease, burns, decubitus ulcers, MRSA considerations.
  • Common tumor categories: lipoma, histiocytoma, mast cell tumor, SCC, melanoma.

Musculoskeletal

  • Hip dysplasia, cranial cruciate rupture, patellar luxation, fracture management basics.
  • Osteosarcoma pattern: away from elbow toward knee and lung metastasis tendency.
  • Panosteitis, HOD, OCD, osteoarthritis, osteomyelitis, hip luxation, immune-mediated polyarthritis.
  • Masticatory muscle myositis diagnosis and immunosuppressive treatment.

Nervous and Respiratory

  • IVDD types I and II with neurologic grading and surgery thresholds.
  • Degenerative myelopathy, FCE, meningitis (septic and immune), GME, seizure disorders, vestibular disease, myasthenia gravis.
  • Kennel cough/CIRDC, distemper, bacterial pneumonia, pneumothorax, brachycephalic airway syndrome.
  • Collapsing trachea, laryngeal paralysis, nasal disease, pleuritis, reverse sneezing, chronic bronchitis.

Ophthalmology

  • Cataracts, glaucoma, uveitis, keratoconjunctivitis sicca, corneal ulcer rules.
  • Diabetes-associated cataract risk and pressure management priorities in glaucoma.

2.4 Small Animal - Feline Core Focus

  • Top chronic diseases: CKD, hyperthyroidism, diabetes mellitus, HCM, FLUTD.
  • Emergency focus: urethral obstruction, hyperkalemia ECG changes, catheterization and post-obstruction monitoring.
  • Cardiology focus: HCM with arterial thromboembolism risk.
  • GI/hepatobiliary focus: triaditis overlap (pancreatitis, cholangitis/cholangiohepatitis, IBD).
  • Infectious focus: FeLV, FIV, FIP, feline URI complex (herpesvirus/calicivirus), panleukopenia.
  • Oncology focus: lymphoma, mammary carcinoma behavior, oral inflammatory disease.
  • Endocrine focus: hyperthyroidism diagnostics/treatment (methimazole, radioiodine, surgery), diabetes protocols.
  • Behavior and pain focus: environmental modification for FLUTD and stress-linked disease control.

2.7 Small Ruminants (Sheep and Goats)

Core Internal Medicine

  • Pregnancy toxemia (ketosis): late gestation, multiple fetuses, hypoglycemia/ketonuria; dextrose and propylene glycol, C-section near term.
  • Hypocalcemia and copper disorders (deficiency and toxicity), especially species sensitivity in sheep.
  • Enterotoxemia (Clostridium perfringens C and D), coccidiosis, Johne disease, bloat, ruminal acidosis.
  • Scrapie: reportable prion disease with no treatment.

Hemic, Integumentary, Musculoskeletal, Nervous

  • Haemonchus contortus with FAMACHA-guided management, CLA, CAE, white muscle disease.
  • Orf (contagious ecthyma), footrot, dermatophilosis, ectoparasites, fleece rot.
  • Arthritis patterns: septic arthritis/navel ill and CAE arthropathy.
  • Neuro priorities: PEM, listeriosis, tetanus, rabies, meningeal worm, CAE encephalitis in kids.

Respiratory, Urinary, Reproductive, Multisystemic

  • OPP, Mannheimia/Pasteurella pneumonia, nasal bots, CCPP reportable concern.
  • Obstructive urolithiasis in castrated males; prevention with calcium:phosphorus balance and ammonium chloride.
  • Abortion causes: Chlamydia, Campylobacter, Toxoplasma, Brucella, Coxiella (Q fever).
  • Mastitis/metritis/prolapse/infertility frameworks.
  • Reportable and public health concerns: Bluetongue, FMD, PPR, zoonotic disease cluster.

2.8 Porcine (Swine)

  • Cardiovascular/endocrine anchors: mulberry heart disease, pericarditis, congenital hypothyroidism.
  • GI anchors: PED, TGE, rotavirus, colibacillosis (including edema disease), salmonellosis, swine dysentery, Lawsonia, gastric ulcers.
  • Hemic/reportable anchors: classical swine fever, African swine fever, Mycoplasma suis anemia.
  • Integument anchors: greasy pig disease, sarcoptic mange, erysipelas (diamond lesions).
  • Musculoskeletal anchors: osteochondrosis, Mycoplasma arthritis, erysipelas arthritis.
  • Nervous anchors: Strep suis meningitis, edema disease, pseudorabies, salt toxicity/water deprivation.
  • Respiratory anchors: PRRS, Mycoplasma pneumonia, influenza, APP, atrophic rhinitis, Glasser disease.
  • Reproductive anchors: PRRS, parvovirus, pseudorabies, leptospirosis, Brucella suis, MMA syndrome.
  • Multisystemic anchors: PCV2/PCVAD, FMD differential, vesicular diseases, trichinellosis, mycotoxicosis, iron deficiency anemia in piglets.

2.9 Poultry

  • Cardiovascular/metabolic: ascites syndrome, sudden death syndrome, cage layer fatigue.
  • GI disease set: necrotic enteritis, coccidiosis, histomoniasis, salmonellosis, ulcerative enteritis, sour crop.
  • Hemic/neoplastic set: Marek disease, lymphoid leukosis, avian leukosis complex.
  • Integument and flock behavior: fowl pox, mites, feather pecking, cannibalism.
  • Nervous/reportable set: Newcastle disease, avian influenza (LPAI/HPAI), avian encephalomyelitis, vitamin E/selenium deficiency, botulism.
  • Respiratory set: infectious bronchitis, infectious laryngotracheitis, Mycoplasma infections, infectious coryza, aspergillosis, airsacculitis.
  • Urinary and reproductive set: gout, egg peritonitis, egg binding, salpingitis, production drop differentials.
  • Multisystemic set: Gumboro disease, egg drop syndrome, colibacillosis, vitamin deficiency syndromes.

2.10 Aquatics (Fish)

  • Integument priorities: ich, saprolegnia, columnaris, Aeromonas/Pseudomonas ulcers, fin rot.
  • Hemic/reportable priorities: spring viremia of carp and viral hemorrhagic septicemia.
  • Respiratory priorities: gill parasites and ammonia/nitrite-associated gill injury.
  • Special senses and stress priorities: exophthalmia, spawning stress, egg binding.
  • Multisystemic priorities: oxygen depletion, temperature stress, fish TB (Mycobacterium marinum), KHV, IPN, furunculosis, enteric redmouth.
  • Nutritional priorities: vitamin C/E and essential fatty acid deficiency syndromes.

4.1 Emergency and Critical Care

Shock, CPCR, Triage

  • Shock types: hypovolemic, cardiogenic, distributive/septic, obstructive.
  • Shock signs: perfusion deficits, mentation change, temperature changes, oliguria.
  • Shock fluid increments: dogs 20 to 30 mL/kg, cats 10 to 15 mL/kg, reassess frequently.
  • CPCR basics: airway, breathing, circulation at 100 to 120 compressions/minute.
  • ALS drug set: epinephrine, atropine, vasopressin, lidocaine, amiodarone, selective bicarbonate use.
  • Triage coding: red (immediate), yellow (urgent), green (stable).

Pain and Toxicology

  • Analgesic ladder: opioids, NSAIDs, local blocks, ketamine/gabapentin adjuncts.
  • Rodenticide classes: anticoagulant, bromethalin, cholecalciferol with different antidote/support patterns.
  • Ethylene glycol, acetaminophen, NSAID overdose, xylitol, chocolate, grapes/raisins, lilies, onions/garlic.
  • Lead, zinc, organophosphates/carbamates, permethrin in cats, ivermectin MDR1 toxicity, THC toxicity.
  • Blue-green algae, sago palm, metaldehyde and severe neurologic/hepatic toxicosis workflows.

4.2 Fluids and Electrolytes

  • Deficit formula: body weight (kg) x percent dehydration = liters deficit.
  • Total daily fluid plan: maintenance + deficit replacement + ongoing losses.
  • Hyperkalemia correction: calcium gluconate (stabilize), insulin plus dextrose, bicarbonate when indicated, isotonic fluid support.
  • Hypokalemia safety: max 0.5 mEq/kg/hr and avoid peripheral overload.
  • Hypercalcemia treatment: sodium chloride fluids then loop diuretic after rehydration, treat cause.
  • Hypocalcemia treatment: slow calcium gluconate IV with ECG/HR monitoring.
  • Hypernatremia correction rate limits to avoid cerebral edema; hyponatremia severe cases may need controlled hypertonic support.
  • Acid-base framework: identify primary disorder then treat cause first.

4.3 Anesthesia and Sedation

  • ASA classification and preanesthetic risk stratification are mandatory.
  • Core pre-op workup: CBC/chem/urinalysis, with expanded testing for senior or unstable patients.
  • Premed options: acepromazine, dexmedetomidine, benzodiazepines, opioids, anticholinergics.
  • Induction options: propofol, ketamine combinations, alfaxalone, etomidate, inhalant induction in select cases.
  • Monitoring set: ECG, respiratory pattern, SpO2, EtCO2, BP, temperature, depth indicators.
  • Complication protocols: hypotension, bradycardia, tachycardia, hypoventilation, hypothermia, malignant hyperthermia.
  • Recovery priorities: delayed extubation in brachycephalics, pain scoring, delirium control, thermoregulation.

4.4 Blood Transfusion Medicine

  • Canine blood groups: DEA system with DEA 1.1 importance; first transfusion often tolerated but typing preferred.
  • Feline blood groups: naturally occurring alloantibodies make typing/crossmatch mandatory before first transfusion.
  • Equine Aa/Qa antigens are clinically relevant for neonatal isoerythrolysis.
  • Product choices: whole blood, packed RBCs, fresh frozen plasma, frozen plasma, cryoprecipitate.
  • Administration safety: start slowly for first 15 to 30 minutes and monitor closely.
  • Acute reactions: hemolytic, febrile, allergic, volume overload, citrate toxicity management steps.

4.5 Diagnostic Imaging Pearls

  • Thoracic interpretation: chamber enlargement clues and pulmonary pattern classification.
  • Pleural disease recognition: effusion and pneumothorax signatures.
  • GDV compartmentalization pattern and intestinal plication clues for obstruction.
  • Urolith radiopacity patterns and contrast-study decision points.
  • Musculoskeletal signatures: hip dysplasia criteria, osteosarcoma aggressive mixed pattern, panosteitis medullary opacity.

4.6 Clinical Pathology Patterns

  • CBC patterns: stress leukogram, regenerative vs degenerative left shift, leukocytosis vs leukopenia.
  • Anemia framework: regenerative vs non-regenerative with reticulocytes and smear findings (spherocytes, Heinz bodies).
  • Chemistry framework: azotemia triage, hepatocellular vs cholestatic enzyme profiles, bilirubin interpretation.
  • Glucose disorders: diabetes confirmation, hypoglycemia differential ladder.
  • Protein patterns: hypoalbuminemia causes, hyperglobulinemia patterns.
  • Urinalysis framework: USG interpretation by species, UPC thresholds, crystal and cast significance.

5. Ethics, Practice Management, Public Health

VCPR, ELDU, Professional Conduct

  • Valid VCPR requires responsibility, patient familiarity, availability/coverage, follow-up, and client agreement.
  • ELDU allowed only under valid VCPR and AMDUCA constraints.
  • Food-animal ELDU prohibitions and withdrawal-time responsibilities are high-yield legal points.
  • Informed consent, confidentiality, record quality, and controlled-substance security are legal standards.
  • Reportable disease duties, abuse reporting laws, and proper prescription writing requirements.

Practice Management

  • Financial literacy: balance sheet, income statement, cash flow, current ratio, debt-to-equity, ROI.
  • Inventory controls: turnover optimization, reorder points, pricing logic.
  • HR essentials: legal hiring, retention strategy, safety compliance.
  • Client communication: estimates, difficult conversations, complaint resolution workflows.

Public Health, One Health, Food-Animal Medicine

  • Zoonotic core set: rabies, leptospirosis, brucellosis, toxoplasmosis, toxocariasis, hookworm, echinococcus, giardia, cryptosporidium, salmonella, campylobacter, bartonella, ringworm, psittacosis, plague, Q fever, orf, M marinum, MRSA, influenza.
  • One Health pillars: zoonotic surveillance, antimicrobial resistance, food safety, environmental health, emergency preparedness.
  • Food-animal biosecurity and stewardship: quarantine, all-in/all-out, VFD compliance, residue prevention, documented withdrawal timing.
  • Animal welfare: five freedoms, welfare scoring, AVMA-consistent euthanasia method selection and confirmation of death.

6.2 Diagnostic Approach Templates

  • PU/PD (dogs): history + CBC/chem/UA + culture, then endocrine/renal/infectious differentiation and imaging when needed.
  • Equine colic: history, exam, reflux quantification, rectal palpation, abdominocentesis, surgery criteria.
  • Chronic diarrhea (dogs/cats): fecal workup, empiric deworming, diet trial, bloodwork, imaging, biopsy for persistent disease.
  • Abortion storm (ruminants): isolate herd mates, collect fetus/placenta/maternal samples, submit PCR/culture/serology panels, update biosecurity/vaccination.
  • Equine lameness: rest exam, dynamic gait exam, flexion tests, distal-to-proximal blocks, radiographs/advanced imaging.

6.3 Treatment Algorithm Summaries

  • DKA (dogs/cats): fluids first, insulin CRI or interval protocol, glucose monitoring, dextrose transition threshold, potassium/phosphorus correction, trigger control.
  • GDV: large-bore IV access, shock doses, gastric decompression, urgent surgery, post-op arrhythmia surveillance.
  • CHF management: oxygen and diuresis acutely, thoracocentesis when indicated, chronic cardiology regimen and monitoring.
  • Feline urethral obstruction: stabilize, sedate/anesthetize, catheterize, correct potassium, analgesia, alpha-blocker, recurrence planning.
  • Bovine milk fever: slow IV calcium borogluconate with monitoring, adjunct support, herd-level prevention strategy.

Critical Contraindications/Cautions

  • Ketamine caution in cats with significant hypertrophic cardiomyopathy.

7.2 Emergency Drug Quick Reference

  • Cardiopulmonary arrest: epinephrine 0.01 mg/kg IV low dose, atropine 0.04 mg/kg IV, vasopressin 0.8 U/kg IV, lidocaine 2 mg/kg IV for ventricular arrhythmias.
  • Anaphylaxis: epinephrine 0.01 to 0.02 mg/kg IM (1:1000), diphenhydramine, dexamethasone support.
  • Seizures: diazepam or midazolam first line, levetiracetam loading option, phenobarbital escalation.
  • Hyperkalemia: calcium gluconate 10 percent, regular insulin plus dextrose, bicarbonate when indicated.
  • Hypoglycemia: controlled dextrose bolus/dilution strategy and oral sugar rescue when no IV access.
  • Eclampsia/hypocalcemia: slow calcium gluconate IV to effect with cardiac monitoring.
  • Acute severe pain: hydromorphone/fentanyl options, ketamine adjunct in selected severe cases.

7.4 Clinical Calculation Formulas

  • BSA estimate (dogs/cats): (kg^0.67 x 10.1) / 100 for chemo contexts.
  • RER: 70 x (kg^0.75); adjust for activity and body-condition goals.
  • Fluid deficit (L): body weight (kg) x dehydration percent.
  • Corrected calcium: measured calcium - albumin + 3.5 (screening estimate).
  • Anion gap: (Na + K) - (Cl + HCO3).
  • FeNa: [(Urine Na x Serum Cr) / (Serum Na x Urine Cr)] x 100.
  • Predicted PCV response and blood-volume rules of thumb (dogs about 90 mL/kg, cats about 60 mL/kg).
  • Breeding date shortcuts: mare minus 1 month plus 7 days, cow minus 3 months plus 10 days.

8. Pattern Recognition and Commonly Confused Concepts

Side-by-Side Comparisons

  • Addison vs Cushing in dogs: age profile, electrolyte pattern, stress leukogram presence, and chronic management differences.
  • Type I vs Type II IVDD: breed bias, onset speed, pathology, and treatment path.
  • LDA vs RDA in cattle: ping location, severity, and volvulus risk context.
  • Regenerative vs non-regenerative anemia: reticulocyte response and marrow interpretation.
  • Struvite vs calcium oxalate uroliths: urine pH associations, dissolution potential, and recurrence prevention.
  • Rabies post-exposure action by vaccination status.
  • EHV-1 vs EVA abortion clue set in horses (autolysis and associated signs).

Classic Pattern Triggers

  • Young unvaccinated dog with hemorrhagic diarrhea plus leukopenia: think parvovirus.
  • Postpartum recumbent cow with S-shaped neck: think milk fever.
  • Male cat straining without urine: think urethral obstruction and hyperkalemia risk.
  • Large dog with distended abdomen and non-productive retching: think GDV.
  • Feedlot calf post-transport with fever/cough: think BRDC.
  • Geriatric cat losing weight despite appetite: think hyperthyroidism.
  • Dog with pot-belly, alopecia, PU/PD: think Cushing disease.
  • Sheep with bottle jaw and pallor: think Haemonchus burden.
  • Pig with diamond skin lesions: think erysipelas.
  • Poultry flock with high mortality and neurologic/respiratory signs: think reportable viral differential.
